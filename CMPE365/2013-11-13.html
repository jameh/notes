Consider this Problem¶

\(n\) persons, \(n\) tasks,

\(w(p_i,t_j)=\) cost of assigning person \(i\) to task \(j\).

Find minimum cost assignment

This can be drawn as either a matrix or a bipartite graph.

Complete bipartite graph, all edges have weight \(\geq\) 0. We want a perfect matching, i.e. one that includes every vertex.

A feasible vertex labelling:

\[l: V\to\mathbb N\]

\(\forall i,j\), \(l(p_i)+l(t_j)\leq w(p_i,t_j)\)

Let \(G_l\) be the set of edges where equality holds. \(l(p_i)+l(t_j)=w(p_i,t_j)\)

Theorem

Suppose \(l\) is feasible & \(G_l\) contains a complete matching \(M\). Then \(M\) is a minimum cost matching.

Proof:

Let \(M'\) be any complete matching. Then

\[\begin{split}w(M')&=\sum_{(p_i,t_j)\in M'}w(p_i,t_j)\\ &\geq\sum_{(p_i,t_j)\in M'}l(p_i)+l(t_j)\\ &=\sum_{p_i\in M'}l(p_i)+\sum_{t_j\in M'}l(t_j)\\ &=w(M)\end{split}\]

Therefore, \(M\) is optimal.
