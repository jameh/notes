Given a finite alphabet homogeneous Markov Chain \(\{X_i\}\) with alphabet

\[\cal X = \{1,2,...,m\}\]

and state transition probability matrix

\[Q_{|\cal X|\times |\cal X|}=[p_{ij}]\]

where \(p_{ij}:=P[X_n=j|X_{n-1}=i]\), \(i,j \in \cal X\)

then the probability distribution vector,

\[\Pi=(\Pi_1,\Pi_2,...,\Pi_m)\]

such that \(\Pi_l\geq 0\), \(l=1,2,...m\) and \(\sum_{l=1}^m \Pi_l=1\)

\[\Pi=\Pi Q\]

Example

Homogeneous Binary Markov Chain

\[\cal X=\{0,1\}\]\[\begin{split}Q_{2\times 2}=[p_{ij}]=\left[\matrix{p_00 & p_01 \\ p_10 & p_11}\right]\end{split}\]\[\begin{split}= \left[\matrix{1-\alpha & \alpha \\ \beta & \1-\beta}\right]\end{split}\]\[.. image::\]

Solve for \(\Pi=(\Pi_1,\Pi_2)\):

\[\Pi = \Pi Q\]\[\begin{split}(\Pi_0,1-\Pi_0)=(\Pi_0,1-\Pi_0)\left(\matrix{1-\alpha & \alpha \\ \beta & \1-\beta}\right)\end{split}\]\[\implies \Pi_0=\frac{\beta}{\alpha+\beta}\]\[\Pi_1=1-\Pi_0\]

therefore,

\[\Pi=(\Pi_0,\Pi_1)=(\frac{\beta}{\alpha+\beta},\frac{\alpha}{\alpha+\beta})\]

So if,

\[P[X_1=0]=\Pi_0=1-P[X_1=1]\]

then the MC is stationary.

Entropy Rate:¶

For a DMS, we defined the source entropy as the entropy of any RV in the DMS. (since all its RVs are iid.)

Question:

How might we define the notion of entropy for a source with memory?

Definition

The entropy rate of a source \(\{X_i\}_{i=1}^\infty\) with alphabet \(\cal X\) is denoted by \(H(\cal X)\) (or \(H_\infty(X)\) or \(H(X_\infty)\)) and defined by:

\[H(\cal X):=\lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{1}{n}H(X_1,X_2,...X_n)\]

provided that the limit exists.

Note

For a DMS, we know that

\[H(X_1,X_2,...,X_n)=H(X_1)+H(X_2)+...+H(X_n)\]

by independence

\[=nH(X_1)\]

by identical distribution

therefore,

\[H(\cal X)=H(X_1)\]

Lemma

For a stationary source, \(\{X_i\}_{i=1}^\infty\), the conditional entropy, \(H(X_n|X_{n-1},...H_1)\) is decreasing in \(n\), and has a limit:

\[H'(\cal X):=\lim_{n\to \infty}H(X_n|X_{n-1},...X_1)\]\[H'(\cal X):=\lim_{n\to \infty}H(X_n|X^{n-1})\]

Proof:

\[H(X_n|X_{n-1},...,X_1)\leq H(X_n|X_{n-1},...,X_2)\]

since conditioning reduces entropy

\[=H(X_n,...X_2)-H(X_{n-1},...,X_2)\]

by chain rule for entropy

And, by stationarity,

\[= H(X_{n-1},...,X_1)-H(X_{n-2},...,X_1)\]\[=H(X_{n-1}|X^{n-2})\]

by the chain rule.

Since \(H(C_n|X^{n-1})\) is a decreasing sequence in \(n\), and is bounded below (by 0), it must have a limit:

\[H'(\cal X)=\lim_{n\to\infty} H(X_n|X_{n-1})\]

Theorem

Cesaro-Mean Theorem

If \(a_n\to_{n\to\infty} a\), and \(b_n=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^\infty a_i\), then

\[b_n\to_{n\to\infty} a\]

Proof pg 76 in Textbook

Theorem

For a stationary source \(\{X_1\}_{i=1}^\infty\) with alphabet \(\cal X\), its entropy rate \(H(\cal X)\) always exists, and is given by:

\[H(\cal X)=H'(\cal X)\]\[=\lim_{n\to \infty}H(X_n|X_{n-1},...,X_1)\]

Proof:

\[\frac{1}{n}H(X_1,...,X_n)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n H(C_1|X^{i-1})\]

by chain rule.

But, we know that \(H(X_i|X^{i-1})\) decreases with \(i\), and converges to \(H'(\cal X)\) as \(i\to\infty\)

So by the Cesaro-Mean Theorem,

\[H(\cal X)=H'(\cal X)\]

Note

For any \(n\),

\[H(\cal X)\leqH(X_n|X^{n-1})\]

and

\[\frac{1}{n}H(X_1,...,X_n)\]

is decreasing in \(n\).

Example

Consider the stationary binary Markov Chain

\[H(\cal X)=\lim_{n\to\infty}H(X_n|X^{n-1})\]\[= \lim_{n\to\infty}H(X_n|X_{n-1})\]\[=H(X_2|X_1)\]\[=-\sum_{i\in\cal X}\sum_{j\in\cal X}P[X_2=j|X_1=i]P[X_1=i]\log_2 P[C_2=j|Xi=i]\]\[=-\sum_{i\in\cal X}\sum_{j\in\cal X}p_{ij}\Pi_i\log_2 p_{ij}\]\[=\frac{\beta}{\alpha+\beta}h_b(\alpha)-\frac{\alpha}{\alpha+\beta}h_b(\beta)\]

where \(h_b(c)=\x\log_2 x - (1-x)\log_2 (1-x)\) is the binary entropy function.

Stationary Ergodic Sources¶

Loosely speaking, stationary sources that cannot be separated into different persisting asymptotic modes of behaviour are known as ergodic sources.

A stationary ergodic source satisfies the Law of Large Numbers.c
