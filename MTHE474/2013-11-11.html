Proof of Channel Coding Thm contd, Feedback¶

Recall

Fano Inequality:

\[:label:`1` H(w|\hat w)\leq 1+nRP_e^{(n)}\]
\[:label:`2` I(X^n;Y^n)\leq nC, \forall p_{X^n}\]

(ii) Proof of the Converse Part¶

Need to show that \(\forall\) sequence of \((2^{nR},n)\) codes with

\[\begin{split}R>C=max_{p_X}I(X;Y)\end{split}\]

their \(P_e^{(n)}\) cannot be made arbitrarily small (Equivalently, \(\forall\) codes \((2^{nR},n)\) with \(P_e^{(n)}\to 0\) as \(n\to \infty\), we must have \(R\leq C\)).

Take message \(w\) uniformly dist. over \(\mathcal M=\{1,2,...,2^{nR}\}\)

Then \(P_e^{(n)}=\frac{1}{2^{nR}}\sum_{i=1}^{2nR}λ_i=P[\hat w\neq w]\)

Hence:

\[H(w)=nR\]

since \(w\) is uniform

Recall

\(H(w)=H(w|\hat w)+I(w;\hat w)\)

Therefore,

\[\begin{split}nR=H(w)\\ =H(w|\hat w)+I(w;\hat w)\\ \leq H(w|\hat w)+I(X^n;Y^n)\end{split}\]

where the inequality follows by the Data Processing inequality. (since \(w\to X^n\to Y^n\to \hat w\))

\[\leq 1+nRP_e^{(n)}+nC\]

Therefore,

\[R\leq \frac{1}{n}+P_e^{(n)}R+C\]

Therefore,

\[P_e^{(n)}\geq 1-\frac{1}{nR}-\frac{C}{R}\]

Note

If \(R>C\), \(P_e^{(n)}\) is bounded away from zero for n suff. large.

Note

Channel rehability (reliability?) problem

Feedback¶

At time \(i\), encoder knows previously received channel outputs via feedback links.

\[x_i=f(w,y_1,...,y_i-1)\]

Note

Shannon showed that for a DMC, \(C_{FB}=C\). In general, for a channel with memory, \(C_{FB}\geq C\).

However, in certain channels with memory, feedback does not help increase the capacity of the channel.

Example

For additive noise channels with Memory:

\[\begin{split}Y_i=X_i\oplus Z_i, i=1,2,...\\ \mathcal X=\mathcal Y=\mathcal Z=\{0,1,...,q-1\}\end{split}\]

\(X_i\) independent of \(Z_i\), \(Z_i\) noise processes has memory, then

\[C_{FB}=C\]

There are no sophisticated schemes with feedback.

Lossless Joint Source-Channel Coding Theorem¶

(Section 7.13 in Textbook - no section in online notes)

We consider 2 systems:

  1. Source \(\to\) Encoder \(\to\) Channel \(\to\) Decoder \(\to\) Destination
  2. Source \(\to\) (Source Encoder \(\to\) Channel Encoder) \(\to\) Channel \(\to\) (Channel Decoder \(\to\) Source Decoder) \(\to\) Destination

Clearly (1) is the more general form, which subsumes (2). So, we should be able to achieve better rates with joint Source-Channel schemes over tandem schemes. However, Shannon showed that if the entropy of the source is less than the capacity of the channel, asymptotically, we lose no optimality by using tandem codes.

Caveat

This result only holds for \(n\to\infty\). (1) still goes to 0 asymptotically faster than (2).
