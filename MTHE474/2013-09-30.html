Proof of Shannon-McMillan AEP Theorem¶

Proof of (i) (Forward Achievability):¶

wlog, assume that \(D=2\) (binary codes).

Therefore, \(H_D(x)=H(X)\) (in base 2).

Now, given \(\alpha>0\), \(\epsilon>0\), choose \(\delta\) s.t. \(0<\delta<\alpha\)

and choose \(n\) and \(k(n)\) such that

\[\begin{split}H(X)+\delta<\frac{k}{n}<H(X)+\alpha\end{split}\]

Now increase \(n\) to satisfy \(P[A_\delta^{(n)}]>1-\epsilon\)

(which is possible by (2) of previous theorem)

while increasing \(k\) to satisfy

\[\begin{split}H(X)+\delta<\frac{k}{n}<H(X)+\alpha\end{split}\]

Encoder:¶

  • For every \(\delta\) - typical source \(n\) - tuple \(x^n \in A_\delta^{(n)}\), assign a distinct (not all-zero) \(k\) - tuple on its codeword
  • If \(x^n \notin A_\delta^{(n)}\), assign it to a default (all-zero) codeword;
\[f(x^n) = 0^k = (0,0,...,0)\]

This encoder is UD for \(x^n \in A_\delta^{(n)}\).

Such an encoder exists iff

\[\begin{split}2^k \geq |A_\delta^{(n)}|+1 \implies 2^k > |A_\delta^{(n)}|\end{split}\]

But we have chosen \(k\) and \(n\) (sufficiently large) to satisfy

\[\begin{split}\frac{k}{n} > H(X)+\delta \iff 2^k > 2^{n(H(X)+\delta)}\end{split}\]

But we know that \(2^{n(H(X)+\delta)} \geq |A_\delta^{(n)}|\)

(by result (3) of previous theorem)

Therefore, indeed we do have:

\[\begin{split}2^k > |A_\delta^{(n)}|\end{split}\]

Decoder:¶

For codewords in \(B\), map them back to their corresponding \(\delta\) - typical sequences.

For codewords in \(B^c\), map them all to a default non \(\delta\) - typical source sequence \(x_0^n\).

By construction, probability of decoding error:

\[P_e = P[X^n \notin A_\delta^{(n)}]\]\[= 1-P[X^n \in A_\delta^{(n)}]\]\[\begin{split}< \epsilon\end{split}\]

for \(n\) sufficiently large.

Proof of (ii) (Strong) Converse Part¶

Consider a binary \((k,n)\) block code for the source satisfying

\[\begin{split}\frac{k}{n} < H(X)\end{split}\]

Then we can find \(\delta>0\) s.t.

\[\frac{k}{n} \leq H(X)-2\delta\]
(1)\[\implies 2^k = \text{# of possible codewords }\leq 2^{n(H(X)-2\delta)}\]

Let \(C'\) be the set of source \(n\) - tuples \(x^n\) that are encoded one-to-one onto distinct codewords:

\[C'=\{x^n\in\mathcal X^n:g(f(x^n))=x^n\}\]

Note

All the other \(n\) - tuples are mapped many-to-one.

The probability of correct decoding (compliment of docoding error):

For \(n\) sufficiently large, the probability of correct decoding \(P_c\), is given by:

\[P_c=P[X^n \in C']\]\[= \sum_{x^n \in C'} P_{X^n}(x^n)\]\[= \sum_{x^n \in C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}} P_{X^n}(x^n) + \sum_{x_n \in C' \cap [A_\delta^{(n)}]^c} P_{X^n}(x^n)\]\[\leq \sum_{x^n \in C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}}P_{X^n}(x^n) + \sum_{x_n \in [A_\delta^{(n)}]^c} P_{X^n}(x^n)\]

The second term has upper bound:

\[\begin{split}\sum_{x_n \in [A_\delta^{(n)}]^c} P_{X^n}(x^n) < \frac{\epsilon}{2}\end{split}\]

for \(n\) suff. large. (by (2) of previous theorem)

So,

\[\sum_{x^n \in C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}}P_{X^n}(x^n) + \sum_{x_n \in [A_\delta^{(n)}]^c} P_{X^n}(x^n)\]\[\begin{split}< |C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}|[max_{x^n\in C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}}P_{X^n}(x^n)] + \frac{\epsilon}{2}\end{split}\]\[\begin{split}< |C'\cap A_\delta^{(n)}|[max_{x^n\in A_\delta^{(n)}}P_{X^n}(x^n)] + \frac{\epsilon}{2}\end{split}\]\[\begin{split}< |C'|*2^{-n(H(X)-\delta)} + \frac{\epsilon}{2}\end{split}\]

(1) then implies:

\[|C'| \leq 2^k \leq 2^{n(H(X)-\delta)}\]
(2)\[\implies |C'| \leq 2^{n(H(X)-\delta)}\]

So,

\[\begin{split}P_c < |C'|*2^{n(H(X)-\delta)} + \frac{\epsilon}{2}\end{split}\]

and by (2):

\[= 2^{-n\delta}+\frac{\epsilon}{2}\]\[\begin{split}< \frac{\epsilon}{2} + \frac{\epsilon}{2} = \epsilon\end{split}\]

Therefore,

\[\begin{split}P_c = 1-P_e < \epsilon\end{split}\]

for \(n\) sufficiently large.

\[\begin{split}\iff P_e > 1-\epsilon\end{split}\]
