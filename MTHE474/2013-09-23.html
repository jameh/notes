Definition

Markov Propery

\((X,Y,Z) \sim^d p_{XYZ}\)

We say \(X,Y,Z\) have the Markov property or form a Markov Chain (MC) in that order, if:

\(X \perp\perp Z | Y \iff p_{XZ|Y}(x,z|y) = p_{X|Y}(x|y)*p_{Z|Y}(z|y)\) \(\forall (x,y,z)\)

We write \(X \to Y \to Z\).

Example

All random variables are binary-valued with common alphabet \(\{0,1\}\). Random variables \(X_0\), \(V_1\), and \(V_2\) are independent of each other.

\(P[V_1 = 1] = P[V_2 = 1] = \epsilon\) for \((0 \leq \epsilon \leq 1)\)

\(P[X_0 = 1] = q\) for \((0 \leq q \leq 1)\)
