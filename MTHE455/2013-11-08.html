Properties of Poisson Process - Superpositioning & Thinning¶

Superposition of Poisson Process¶

Let \(\{N_1(t): t\geq 0\}\) and \(\{N_1(t): t\geq 0\}\) be independent Poisson processes with rates \(λ_1,λ_2\) resp. Let \(N(t)=N_1(t)+N_2(t)\), \(t\geq 0\).

Then \(\{N(t): t\geq 0\}\) is a Poisson process with rate \(λ_1+λ_2\).

  1. \(N(0)=N_1(0)+N_2(0)=0+0=0\)
  2. We have that
\[\begin{split}N(t_2)-N(t_1)&=N_1(t_2)+N_2(t_2)-N_1(t_1)-N_2(t_2)\\ &=(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_2))+(N_2(t_2)-N_2(t_2))\\ &\sim Poisson(λ_1(t_2-t_1)) + Poisson(λ_2(t_2-t_1))\\ &= Poisson((λ_1+λ_2)(t_2-t_1))\end{split}\]

This distribution depends on \(t_1\) and \(t_2\) only through \(t_2-t_1\), so \(N\) has stationary increments.

Let \(t_1<t_2<t_3<t_4\).

Then

\[\begin{split}N(t_2)-N(t_1)=(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_1))+(N_2(t_2)-N_2(t_1))\\ N(t_4)-N(t_3)=(N_1(t_4)-N_1(t_3))+(N_2(t_4)-N_2(t_3))\end{split}\]

Everything in first line is independent of everything in second line, so \(N\) has stationary and independent increments.

  1. \(N(t)=N_1(t)+N_2(t)\sim Poisson(λ_1t)+Poisson(λ_2t)=Poisson((λ_1+λ_2)t)\)

Thinning a Poisson Process¶

Let \(\{N(t): t\geq 0\}\) be a Poisson process with rate \(λ\). Suppose, independently from event to event, that each event is a type “1” event with probability \(p\) and a type “2” event with probability \(1-p\), with \(0<p<1\) fixed.

Let

\[\begin{split}N_1(t)=\text{# of type 1 events in }[0,t]\\ N_2(t)=\text{# of type 2 events in }[0,t]\end{split}\]

Then \(\{N_1(t):t\geq 0\}\) and \(\{N_2(t):t\geq 0\}\) are Poisson processes, with rates \(λp\) and \(λ(1-p)\) respectively, and these “thinned” processes are independent.

Consider \(\{N_1(t):t\geq 0\}\),

\[\begin{split}P(N_1(h)=1)&=(λh+o(h))p+o(h)\\ &=λph+o(h)\\ P(N_1(h)=0)&=1-λh+o(h)+(λh+o(h))(1-p)\\ &=1 - λph + o(h)\end{split}\]

So \(N_1\) satisfies condition (iii) of definition 2.

Also clearly \(N_1(0)=0\)

It remains to show stationary and independent increments.

Stationary Increments¶

Let \(t_1<t_2\).

\[\begin{split}&P(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_1)=k)\\ &=\sum_{n=k}^\infty P(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_1)=k|N(t_2)-N(t_1)=n)\times P(N(t_2)-N(t_1)=n)\\ &=\sum_{n=k}^\infty {n \choose k} p^k(1-p)^{n-k}\frac{(λ(t_2-t_1))^n}{n!}e^{-λ(t_2-t_1)}\\ &=\frac{[λp(t_2-t_1)]^k}{k!}e^{-λp(t_2-t_1)}\sum_{n=k}^\infty \frac{[(1-p)λ(t_2-t_1)]^{n-k}}{(n-k)!}e^{-λ(1-p)(t_2-t_1)}\\ &=\frac{[λp(t_2-t_1)]^k}{k!}e^{-λp(t_2-t_1)}e^{(1-p)λ(t_2-t_1)}e^{-λ(1-p)(t_2-t_1)}\\ &=\frac{[λp(t_2-t_1)]^k}{k!}e^{-λp(t_2-t_1)}\end{split}\]

So \(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_1)\sim Poisson(λp(t_2-t_1))\), which depends on \(t_1\) and \(t_2\) only though \(t_2-t_1\), so \(N_1\) has stationary increments.

Independent Increments¶

Let \(t_1<t_2<t_3<t_4\)

\[P(N_1(t_2)-N_1(t_1)=n_1,N_1(t_4)-N_1(t_3)=n_2)\]

Condition on \(N(t_2)-N(t_1)\) and \(N(t_4)-N(t_3)\).

Then, the conditional probability will factor, and the \(P(N(t_2)-N(t_1)=k_1,N(t_4)-N(t_3)=k_2)\) will also factor, then summing over all \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) factors the above joint probability.

So conditions (i),(ii),(iii) are satisfied to show \(N_1\) is a Poisson process with rate \(λp\). Similar arguments show \(N_2\) is a Poisson process with rate \(λ(1-p)\).

This generalizes to more than 2 types. If you have \(n\) types with probabilities

\[p_1,p_2,...,p_n\]

with \(p_1+...+p_n=1\), we can break this down iteratively one by one

Similarly, summing \(n\) independent Poisson processes with rates \(λ_1,...,λ_n\) yields a Poisson process with rate \(λ_1+...+λ_n\).
