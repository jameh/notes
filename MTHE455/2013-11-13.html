Review of Order Statistics¶

Suppose \(X_1,...,X_n\) are independent, identically distributedm continuous random variables with common pdf \(f\).

The joint pdf of \((X_1,...,X_n)\) is

\[f_n(x_1,...,x_n)=\prod^n_{i=1}f(x_i)\]

The order statistics of \(X_1,...,X_n\) are denoted \(X_{(1)},...,X_{(n)}\), where

\[\begin{split}X_{(1)}&=min(X_1,...,X_n)\\ ...\\ X_{(k)}&=\text{kth smallest of }X_1,...,X_n\\ ...\\ X_{(n)}&=max(X_1,...,X_n)\end{split}\]

Let us consider the joint pdf of the order statistics \(X_{(1)},...,X_{(n)}\)

Call this \(g(x_1,...,x_n)\).

Note

If \(x_1,...,x_n\) do not satisfy \(x_1<x_2<...<x_n\), then \(g(x_1,...,x_n)\) will be 0. So let \(x_1,...,x_n\) be such that \(x_1<x_2<...<x_n\)

Consider

\[\begin{split}P(X_{(i)}\in [x_i,x_i+h),i=1,...,n)\\ =\sum_Π P(X_{Π_i}\in [x_i,x_i+h),i=1,...,n)\end{split}\]

where the sum is over all permutations \((Π_1,...,Π_n)\) of \((1,...,n)\)

\[=n!P(X_i\in[x_i,x_i+h),i=1,...,n)\]

Dividing this by \(h^n\) and letting \(h\to 0\) gives \(g(x_1,...,x_n)\)

So

\[\begin{split}g(x_1,...,x_n)&=n!f_n(x_1,...,x_n)\\ &=n!\prod_{i=1}^n f(x_i)\end{split}\]

Last time, we got that the conditional joint density of the arrival times \(S_1,...,S_n\) given \(N(t)=n\) is

\[h(s_1,...,s_n|N(t)=n)=\frac{n!}{t^n}\]

for \(0<s_1<s_2<...<s_n<t\), and 0 otherwise

Note

\[\frac{n!}{t^n}=n!\prod_{i=1}^n\frac{1}{t}\]

In other words, the conditional distribution of the arrival times \(S_1,...,S_n\) given \(N(t)=n\) is that of the order statistics of \(n\) i.i.d. \(Uniform(0,t)\) random variables.

Example

An insurance company receives claims according to a Poisson process with rate \(λ\). The cost of the ith claim is \(C_i\), where \(C_1,C_2,...\) are independent and identically distributed with mean \(μ\).

Then, in the interval \([0,t]\), the total discounted cost of the claims is

\[D(t)=\sum_1^{N(t)} e^{-αS_i}C_i\]

Where \(S_i\) is the arrival time of the ith claim and \(α\) is the discount rate.

Let us compute \(E[D(t)]\).

Conditioning on \(N(t)\), we have

(1)\[\begin{split}E[D(t)]&=\sum_{n=0}^\infty E[D(t)|N(t)=n]P(N(t)=n)\\ &=\sum_{n=0}^\infty E[\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} e^{-αS_i}C_i|N(t)=n] P(N(t)=n)\end{split}\]

where

\[E[\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} e^{-αS_i}C_i|N(t)=n]=E[\sum_{i=1}^ne^{-αU_{(i)}}C_i]\]

where \(U_{(i)}\) is the ith order statistic from \(U_1,...,U_n\) where \(U_i\) is \(Uniform(0,t)\)

\[\begin{split}&=\sum_{i=1}^n E[C_i]E[e^{-αU_{(i)}}]\\ &=μ\sum_{i=1}^n E[e^{-αU_{(i)}}]\\ &=μΕ[\sum_{i=1}^n e^{-αU_{(i)}}]\\ &=μE[\sum_{i=1}^n e^{-αU_i}]\\ &=μnE[e^{-αU_1}]\\ &=μn\int_0^t \frac{1}{t}e^{-αu}du\\ &=μn\frac{1}{t}\frac{1}{α}(1-e^{-αt})\end{split}\]

Putting this back into (1) gives

\[\begin{split}E[D(t)]&=μ\frac{1}{αt}(1-e^{-αt})E[N(t)]\\ &=\frac{μλ}{α}(1-e^{-αt})\end{split}\]

Example

Filtered Poisson Process

Suppose

\[X(t)=\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)}f(t,S_i,Y_i)\]

where \(\{N(t):t\geq 0\}\) is Poisson process with rate \(λ\), \(S_1,...,S_n\) are the arrival times, and \(Y_1,Y_2,...\) are independent and identically distributed random variables.

Let us compute \(E[s^{X(t)}]\).

We have

\[\begin{split}E[s^{X(t)}]&=E[s^{\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)}f(t,S_i,Y_i)}]\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty E[s^{\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)}f(t,S_i,Y_i)}|N(t)=k]P(N(t)=k)\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty E[s^{\sum_{i=1}^k f(t,U_{(i)},Y_i)}]P(N(t)=k)\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty E[s^{\sum_{i=1}^k f(t,U_i,Y_i)}]P(N(t)=k)\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty E[\prod_{i=1}^k s^{f(t,U_i,Y_i)}]P(N(t)=k)\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty \prod_{i=1}^k E[s^{f(t,U_i,Y_i)}]P(N(t)=k)\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^\infty E[s^{f(t,U_i,Y_i)}]^k P(N(t)=k)\\ =G_{N(t)}(E[s^{f(t,U_1,Y_1)}])\end{split}\]

where \(G_{N(t)}(s)\) is the generating function of \(N(t)\). Since \(N(t)\) is \(Poisson(λt)\), \(G_{N(t)}\) is

\[\begin{split}\sum_{k=0}^\infty s^k\frac{(λt)^k}{k!}e^{-λt}\\ =e^{-λt}e^{sλt}=e^{λt(s-1)}\end{split}\]
