Kolmogorov Equations¶

\[\begin{split}P(0\text{ transitions by time }h | X(0)=i)&=1-&v_ih+&o(h)\\ P(\text{exactly 1 transition by time }h | X(0)=i)&= &v_ih+&o(h)\\ P(2\text{ or more transitions by time }h|X(0)=i)&= & &o(h)\end{split}\]

From these we can get

(1)\[P(X(h)=i|X(0)=i) = 1-v_ih+o(h)+o(h)\]

because we exclusively either made 0 transitions, or 2 or more transitions.

For \(j\neq i\),

\[\begin{split}P&(X(h)=j|X(0)=i)\\ &=P(X(h)=k|\text{1 transition},X(0)=i)P(\text{1 transition}|X(0)=i) + P(X(h)=k|\text{2 or more transitions})P(\text{2 or more transitions}|X(0)=i)\\ &=p_{ij}(v_ih+o(h))+o(h)\end{split}\]

So,

(2)\[P(X(h)=j|X(0)=i)=p_{ij}v_ih+o(h)\]

Let \(p_{ij}(t)=P(X(t)=j|X(0)=i)\). This is analogous to the n-step transition probability in discrete-time Markov chains. But in continuous-time, it is a function of the continuous index \(t\).

\(p_{ij}(t)\) is called the transition probability function from \(i\) to \(j\).

Let \(P(t)=(p_{ij}(t))_{i,j\in S}\) (i.e. \(P(t)_{ij}=p_{ij}(t)\)) be the (matrix-valued) transition probability function.

Note

\(P(0)\) is the \(S\times S\) identity matrix.

Kolmogorov’s Backward Equations¶

Consider \(p_{ij}(t+h)\). We have:

\[\begin{split}p_{ij}(t+h)&=P(X(t+h)=j|X(0)=i)\\ &=\sum_{k\in S}P(X(t+h)=j|X(h)=k, X(0)=i)P(X(h)=k|X(0)=i)\\ &=\sum_{k\in S}P(X(t)=j|X(0)=k)P(X(h)=k|X(0)=i)\\ &=\sum_{k\in S}p_{kj}(t)P(X(h)=k|X(0)=i)\end{split}\]

Now use (1) and (2), separate out case \(k=i\):

We get:

\[p_{ij}(t+h)=p_{ij}(t)(1-v_ih+o(h))+\sum_{k\neq i}p_{kj}(t)(p_{ik}v_ih+o(h))\]

or

\[\frac{p_{ij}(t+h)-p_{ij}(t)}{h}=-v_ip_{ij}(t)+\sum_{k\neq i}p_{kj}(t)p_{ik}v_i+\frac{o(h)}{h}\]

Letting \(h\to 0\), we get

\[\begin{split}p'_{ij}(t)&=-v_ip_{ij}(t)+\sum_{k\neq i}q_{ik}p_{kj}(t)\\ p'_{ij}(t)&=\sum_{k\in S}g_{ik}p_{kj}(t)\end{split}\]

where

\[\begin{split}g_{ik}=\begin{cases} q_ik & k\neq i\\ -v_i & k=i \end{cases}\end{split}\]

is the \((i,j)\) entry of the generator matrix \(G\).

If we let \(P'(t)=((p_{ij}'(t)))_{i,j\in S}\), then

\[P'(t)_{ij}=(GP(t))_{ij}\]

or in matrix form,

(3)\[P'(t)=GP(t)\]

(3) with initial condition \(P(0)=I_{S\times S}\) are Kolmogorov’s Backward Differential Equations

The solution is

\[\begin{split}P(t)&=I+tG+\frac{(tG)^2}{2}+\frac{(tG)^3}{3!}+...+\frac{(tG)^n}{n!}+...\\ &=e^{tG}\end{split}\]

Verify:

\[\begin{split}P'(t)&=G+tG^2+\frac{t^2}{2}G^3+...+\frac{t^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}G^n+...\\ &=G(I+tG+\frac{(tG)^2}{2}+\frac{(tG)^3}{3!}+...+\frac{(tG)^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}+...)\\ &=GP(t)\end{split}\]

It is also clear that you get \(I\) when \(t=0\).

Important Remark

Note that \(G\) determines \(P(t)\) for all \(t\).

\(G\) is the counterpart of \(P\) in discrete-time.

Stationary Distributions¶

Definition

An \(|S|\) dimensional row vector \(Π\) with \(Π_i\geq 0\) for all \(i\) and \(\sum_{i\in S}Π_i=1\) is a stationary distribution if

\[Π=ΠP(t)\]

for any \(t\geq 0\).

If \(X(0)\) has distribution \(Π\), then

\[\begin{split}P(X(t)=j)&=\sum_{i\in S}P(X(t)=j|X(0)=i)Πi\\ &=\sum_{i\in S}p_{ij}(t)Π_i\\ &=(ΠP(t))_j\\ &=Π_j\end{split}\]

Now we can see that \(Π\) is a stationary distribution iff

\[\begin{split}Π&=ΠP(t)&\text{ for all }t\geq 0\\ \iff Π&=Π\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(tG)^n}{n!}&\text{ for all }t\geq 0\\ \iff 0&=Π\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(tG)^n}{n!}&\text{ for all }t\geq 0\\ \iff 0&=ΠG^n&\text{ for all }n\geq 0\\ \iff 0&=ΠG\end{split}\]

This is the continuous-time counterpart of the discrete-time global balance equations.

\[0=ΠG\]

are called the global balance equations. The \(j\) th equation is:

\[0=\sum_{i\in S}Π_ig_{ij}\]

or

\[Π_jv_j = \sum_{i\neq j}Π_iq_{ij}\]

“flow out of \(j\)” = “flow into \(j\)”.
