Fundamental Theorm of Algebra & Max Modulus Theorems¶

Recall

Liouville’s Theorem

If \(f\) is entire and bounded (\(|f(z))|\leq M, \forall x\in\mathbb C\)), then \(f\) is constant.

Theorem

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

If \(P(z)\) is a polynomial of degree \(n\geq 2\) over \(\mathbb C\), then \(P(z)\) factors completely as a product of linear factors.

Proof:

It is enough to show

\[P(α)=0\]

for some \(α\in \mathbb C\) because if \(P(α)=0\), polynomial long division can show

\[P(z)=q(z)(z-α)\]

where \(q(z)\) is degree \(n-1\) polynomial.

Proof that root exists by contradiction:

Suppose \(P(z)\) has not roots. Then \(f(z)=\frac{1}{P(z)}\) is holomorphic everywhere.

Since

\[|P(z)|\geq |a_n||z|^n-|a_{n-1}||z|^{n-1}-...-|a_1||z|-|a_0|\]

Thus, \(\exists ρ>0\) such that

\[|P(z)|\geq \frac{|a_n|}{2}|z|^n\]

for \(|z|>0\)

So

\[\begin{split}|f(z)|&=\frac{1}{|P(z)|}\\ &\leq \frac{2}{|a_n||z|^n}\\ &\leq \frac{2}{|a_n|ρ^n}\end{split}\]

for \(|z|\geq ρ\)

Let

\[M=max_{z\in \bar D_ρ(0)}|f(z)|\]

Then we have:

\[|f(z)|\leq Max(M, \frac{2}{|a_n|ρ^n})\]

Max Modulus Theorems for Holomorphic and Harmonic Functions¶

Suppose \(f:D\to \mathbb C\) is holomorphic and \(D\) contains a closed disk centered at \(z_0\) of radius \(r\).

Then

\[f(z_0)=\frac{1}{2π}\int_0^{2π}f(z_0+re^{iθ})dθ\]

Proof:¶

Let \(γ:[0,2π]\to D\) by CIF

\[\begin{split}f(z_0)&=\frac{1}{2πi}\int_γ\frac{f(w)}{w-z_0}dw\\ &=\frac{1}{2πi}\int_0^{2π}\frac{f(γ(t))}{γ(t)-z_0}γ'(t)dt\\ &=\frac{1}{2π}\int_0^{2π}f(z_0+re^{it})dt\end{split}\]

Theorem

Max Modulus (Local Version)

If \(f:D\to \mathbb C\) is holomorphic and \(\exists\) a \(z_0\in D\) that is a local max: \(\exists ρ>0\) such that if \(z\in D_ρ(z_0)\), \(|f(z)|\leq |f(z_0)|\), then \(f\) is constant on \(D_ρ(z_0)\).

Lemma

Suppose \(f:D\to\mathbb C\) is holomorphic and \(|f(z)|\) is constant, then \(f(z)\) is constant.

Proof:

First suppose \(|f(z)|=0\) for some \(z\in D\), so \(|f(z)|=0\) on all \(D\), so is \(f(z)\).

So consider

\[C=|f(z)|^2=f(z)\bar f(z)\]

So

\[\bar f(z)=\frac{C}{f(z)}\]

is holomorphic on \(D\). (look at CREs)
