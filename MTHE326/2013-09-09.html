Functions of a Complex Variable¶

Complex differentiability¶

\[f: \Bbb C \to \Bbb C\]

Complex Integrals are more fun¶

Lets us evaluate some real integrals e.g.

\[\int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\sin x}{x}dx\]

3 ways to describe \(\Bbb C\): 1. Arithmetically as a real vector space 2. Algebraic closure of \(\Bbb R\) 3. Geometrically as a real vector space.

Definition

\(z \in \Bbb C\) are such that \(z=x+iy\), \(x,y \in \Bbb R\) where \(i^{2}=-1\).

If \(z = x+iy\),
  • \(Re(z):=x\)
  • \(Im(z):=y\)
  • \(\bar{z}:=x-iy\)

Operations¶

Addition¶

group real & imaginary parts

Multiplication¶

\[(x+iy)(a+ib) = xa + ixb + iya - by\]\[ = xa - yb + i(xb + ya) = (a+ib)(x+iy)\]

Note

multiplication is commutative, as in \(\Bbb R\).

Division¶

\[\frac{x+iy}{a+ib} = \frac{(x_iy)(a-ib)}{a^2+b^2} = \frac{xa+yb + i(ay - bx)}{a^2+b^2}\]

The properties of these operations satisfy the field properties.

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra¶

The polynomial \(f(z) = z^n + a_{n-1}z^{n-1} +...+ a_1 z + a_0\) factors completely under \(\Bbb C\)

Definition

A field \(\Bbb F\) is algebraically closed if every polynomial over \(\Bbb F\) factors completely. \(\Bbb C\) is the smallest such field for \(\Bbb R\); it is its algebraic closure.
