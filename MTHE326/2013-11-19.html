Residue Theorem and Calculation of Residues¶

Proof of Residue Lemma from previous lecture:¶

  1. We have

    \[f(z)=\frac{g(z)}{h(z)}=\frac{a_k(z-z_0)^k+a_{k+1}(z-z_0)^{k+1}+...}{b_k(z-z_0)^k+b_{k+1}(z-z_0)^{k+1}+...}\]

    Where \(a_k,b_k\neq 0\), \(k\in\mathbb Z\).

    Then

    \[f(z)=\frac{a_k+a_{k+1}(z-z_0)+...}{b_k+b_{k+1}(z-z_0)+...}\]

    So we can let \(f(z_0)=\frac{a_k}{b_k}\)

  2. If \(\lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)f(z)=L\), then

    \[\lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)\sum_{n=-\infty}^\infty a_n(z-z_0)^n=L\]

    So

    \[\lim_{z\to z_0}\sum_{n=-\infty}^\infty a_n(z-z_0)^{n+1}=L\]

    (\(n+1\leq 0\) or limit won’t exist)

    \(a_n=0\) for \(n<-1\)

    So \(f\) has, at most, a simpmle pole, and \(a_{-1}=L\).

  3. \(f(z)=\frac{g(z)}{h(z)}\), \(g(z_0)\neq 0\), \(h(z_0)=0\), \(h'(z_0)\neq 0\) then

    \[f(z)=\frac{a_0+a_1(z-z_0)+a_2(z-z_0)^2+...}{b_1(z-z_0)+b_2(z-z_0)^2+...}\]

    \(a_0\neq 0\), \(b_1\neq 0\)

    So

    \[\begin{split}f(z)&=\frac{1}{z-z_0}\frac{a_0+a_1(z-z_0)+a_2(z-z_0)^2+...}{b_0+b_1(z-z_0)+b_2(z-z_0)^2+...}\\ &=\frac{s(z)}{z-z_0}\end{split}\]

    Where

    \[s(z):=\frac{a_0+a_1(z-z_0)+a_2(z-z_0)^2+...}{b_0+b_1(z-z_0)+b_2(z-z_0)^2+...}\]

    is holomorphic at \(z_0\) and \(s(z_0)\neq 0\).

    So:

    \[\begin{split}\lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)f(z)&=\lim_{z\to z_0}s(z)\\ &=s(z_0)\\ &=\frac{a_0}{b_1}\\ &=\frac{g(z_0)}{h'(z_0)}\\ &=Res(f,z_0)\end{split}\]

  4. \(f(z)=\frac{g(z)}{(z-z_0)^2}\), \(g(z_0)\neq 0\) then

    \[\begin{split}f(z)&=\frac{a_0+a_1(z-z_0)+a_2(z-z_0)^2+...}{(z-z_0)^2}\\ &=\frac{a_0}{(z-z_0)^2}+\frac{a_1}{(z-z_0)}+a_2+...\end{split}\]

    So degree two pole and \(Res(f,z_0)=a_1=g'(z_0)\)

  5. Explored in Assignment 11.

Examples

  1. Find residues of \(f(z)=\frac{2}{z^2+1}\) at \(z=\pm i\)

    \[\begin{split}f(z)&=\frac{2}{(z+i)(z-i)}\\ &=\frac{g(z)=\frac{2}{z+i}}{h(z)=(z-i)}\end{split}\]

    \(g(z)\) is holomorphic at \(z=i\), not zero and \(h(z)\) is zero at \(z=i\).

    By Residue Lemma 3,

    \[\begin{split}Res(f,i)&=\frac{g(i)}{h'(i)}\\ &=\frac{\frac{2}{i+i}}{1}\\ &=-i\end{split}\]

    symmetrically,

    \[\begin{split}f(z)&=\frac{2}{(z+i)(z-i)}\\ &=\frac{g(z)=\frac{2}{z-i}}{h(z)=(z+i)}\end{split}\]

    \(g(z)\) is holomorphic at \(z=-i\), not zero and \(h(z)\) is zero at \(z=-i\).

    So

    \[\begin{split}Res(f,-i)&=\frac{g(-i)}{h'(-i)}\\ &=\frac{\frac{2}{(-i-i)}}{1}\\ &=i\end{split}\]

    Can also use Lemma 2, find \(\lim_{z\to\pm i}(z\mp i)f(z)\)
